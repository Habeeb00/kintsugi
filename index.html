金継ぎ

Something broke.

Not loudly.

The way things break sometimes —
without anyone noticing,
not even you.

In Japan, when a bowl breaks,
they do not throw it away.

They mend the cracks with gold.

金継ぎ — kintsugi.

The bowl is not pretending to be unbroken.

It is showing you
exactly where it broke,
and exactly how it chose to stay.

侘び寂び

The Japanese have a word
for the beauty of imperfect things.

We were not built to be unbroken.
We were built to survive the breaking —
and carry our repairs as proof
that we chose to stay.
